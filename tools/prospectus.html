What is CSIhacks?

CSIhacks is a high-school hackathon built around five tracks — Best Overall, Best AI/ML, Best Fintech, Best Hardware, and Best UI/UX. One day. One room. Real projects, real judges, real prizes.

100+

Who shows up

The Hackers

High-school and early-college students from across the Bay Area — self-taught coders, robotics-club kids, AoPS regulars, and first-time builders looking for a starting line.

The Mentors & Judges

Working engineers, founders, and educators rotating between teams, unsticking projects, and judging final demos. Sponsors are invited to send their own.

The Backers

Run by California Stem Innovators (501(c)(3) sponsorship via Hack Club), with backing from companies like CodeCrafters, PCBWay, Featherless, Wolfram, and Tavily.

The Vibe

Pixel art, late-night soldering, espresso, and a closing ceremony that actually feels like one. Students leave with shipped projects in their portfolio and a network they didn't have that morning.

Why sponsor CSIhacks?

Hackathons are recruiting funnels, product feedback labs, and brand moments — all in one room. Sponsoring CSIhacks puts you in front of a cohort that's about to enter your hiring pipeline, your dev community, and your customer base.

1. Talent pipeline

Meet ambitious high-schoolers years before they're on the market. Send recruiters, mentors, and judges; collect résumés directly.

2. Real product feedback

See your APIs, SDKs, and tools used by motivated developers in real time — and watch what breaks. The bug reports alone are worth it.

3. Brand moments

Logo on-site, on the website, on shirts, in slides, on stage. Your name shows up where the hackers are paying attention.

4. Community goodwill

Sponsoring student hackathons buys long-tail goodwill. The students you back today are the engineers who'll champion you in five years.

What we'll do for you

  • On-stage moments: opening welcome, closing remarks, or a keynote, depending on tier.
  • Custom tracks & awards: design a prize that maps to your product or community.
  • Tabling space: set up a booth, demo your tools, hand out swag, run side-quests.
  • Recruitment access: distribute materials, collect résumés, send engineers as mentors and judges.
  • Brand placement: logo on the website, on event shirts, in the deck, in post-event recap.

Each tier comes with a baseline commitment and an expected level of involvement. Below is the default — we'll happily customize around what your team can give and what you want to get out of being there.
